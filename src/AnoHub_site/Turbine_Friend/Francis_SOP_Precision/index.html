SOP: Mechanical Precision & Alignment

Critical SOP: Mechanical Precision

The "6-Month Rule" & Structural Verification

⚠️ THE TROPICAL FOUNDATION RISK

Context: In humid/tropical environments, concrete absorbs moisture and undergoes thermal expansion/contraction cycles. This causes "Micro-Movements".

  • Result: A unit perfectly aligned in January may be 0.15mm off-center by July.
  • Protocol: You MUST verify alignment every 6 months. Do not assume the foundation is static.
SOP 1: Labyrinth Clearance Checks

The gap between the rotating runner and the stationary seal ring (Labyrinth) determines efficiency and leakage.

1.1 Measurement Procedure

  1. Lock Out: Ensure Unit is locked out and drained.
  2. Access: Enter the draft tube or turbine pit access door.
  3. Define Points: Mark 4 points (Top, Bottom, Left, Right) or 8 points (Clockwise) on the casing.
  4. Measure: Insert Feeler Gauges into the gap. Start small.
    • Range: 0.05mm - 1.00mm.
    • Find the gauge that fits "snug" (drag resistance).
  5. Record: Log values for all points.

1.2 Analysis

  • Uniformity: The deviation between Top/Bottom and Left/Right should not exceed 10%.
  • Rubbing Risk: If any single point is < 0.20mm, the runner is in danger of seizing. Re-align immediately.
SOP 2: Shaft Run-Out Check

Vibration is often just a symptom of a bent shaft or a misaligned coupling.

2.1 Setup

  1. Clean: Polish a section of the shaft near the bearing (remove oil/paint).
  2. Mount: Attach the Magnetic Base of the Dial Indicator to a fixed, non-vibrating point (e.g., bearing housing).
  3. Zero: Place the needle on the shaft surface. Pre-load by 1-2mm. Rotate bezel to "0".

2.2 Rotation & Reading

  1. Rotate: Manually turn the shaft (barring gear or by hand) very slowly.
  2. Observe: Watch the Total Indicator Reading (TIR).
  3. The Limit:
    • Pass: Deviation ≤ 0.05mm.
    • Fail: Deviation > 0.05mm.
Visual Goal: The needle should stay steady. Systematic wobble means the shaft axis is off-center.
Mandatory Tooling

You cannot perform "Expert Maintenance" with just a wrench. These two tools are non-negotiable.

  • 1. Dial Test Indicator (DTI):
    • Precision: 0.01mm (Hundredth of a millimeter).
    • Type: Plunger or Lever type with Magnetic Stand.
  • 2. Precision Feeler Gauge Set:
    • Blades: Steel or Brass.
    • Range: 0.05mm steps up to 1.00mm.
    • Length: Long blades (200mm+) preferred for reaching deep gaps.
