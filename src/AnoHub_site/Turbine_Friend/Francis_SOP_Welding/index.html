Stainless Steel Runner Repair Protocol

STOP. READ THIS FIRST.

  • Hot Work Permit: Obtain permit before welding. Fire extinguishers nearby.
  • Ventilation: Stainless steel welding produces hexavalent chromium fumes (carcinogenic). Use forced ventilation and masks.
  • Eye Protection: Welding helmet with shade 10-12.
  • Electrical Safety: Ensure machine ground. Dry working zone.
Material Matching: Electrode Selection

Why Matching Matters

Runner is typically 13Cr4Ni Martensitic Stainless Steel. Wrong electrode causes galvanic corrosion, cracking, or reduced hardness.

Recommended Electrodes

  • 308L (E308L-16): General cavitation repair. Austenitic, good corrosion resistance.
  • 309L (E309L-16): Joining dissimilar metals. Higher chromium content.
üí° Technical Note: 308L or 309L are preferred‚ÄîNO pre-heating or post-weld heat treatment required.

Welding Parameters

  • Current: 80-120 A (3.2 mm electrode)
  • Polarity: DCEP (Electrode Positive)
  • Interpass Temperature: < 150¬∞C
Profile Template: Preserving Blade Curvature

Making a Profile Gauge

  1. Select Undamaged Face: Find adjacent undamaged blade.
  2. Material: Thick cardboard, plastic, or thin aluminum (1-2 mm).
  3. Copy Profile: Press material against blade, trace exact curve.
  4. Cut Template: Cut along the line.
  5. Label: Record blade number and position.

Using the Template

  1. After grinding: Remove all damaged material.
  2. After welding: Build up 1-2 mm excess metal.
  3. Finish grinding: Use template as guide‚Äîblade must match template.

'Smoothness' Standard: Mirror Effect

Why Surface Finish Matters

Any surface roughness acts as a nucleation point for new cavitation bubbles.

Target: Ra < 1.6 Œºm (Mirror Effect)

Grinding & Polishing Procedure

  1. Rough Grinding (40-60 grit): Remove excess, shape profile.
  2. Medium Grinding (80-120 grit): Smooth out rough marks.
  3. Fine Grinding (180-240 grit): Achieve smooth surface.
  4. Polishing (320-600 grit): Buff to mirror finish.
üí° Technical Note: Always grind parallel to water flow direction (inlet to outlet). Cross-scratches create turbulence.

Visual Inspection

  • Mirror Test: Flashlight at shallow angle must reflect clearly.
  • Fingernail Test: Should feel no bumps or scratches.
Dynamic Balance Warning

Why Balance Matters

Adding metal to one blade creates mass imbalance, causing vibration, bearing wear, shaft fatigue, and seal failure.

Weld Metal ‚Üí Imbalance ‚Üí Centrifugal Force ‚Üí Vibration

Mitigation Strategies

1. Symmetrical Repair (Preferred)

Repair all damaged blades to maintain symmetry.

2. Adding Counterweights

Add same mass to opposite blade (180¬∞ away) in non-critical zone.

3. Runout Check Post-Repair

  1. Mount runner on balancing stand.
  2. Rotate slowly, measure radial runout with dial indicator.
  3. Target: Runout < 0.05 mm at blade tips.
‚ö†Ô∏è CRITICAL: DO NOT add more than 100g of metal to a single blade without consulting a balancing specialist.
Material & Tool Checklist
  • Electrodes: E308L-16 or E309L-16 (3.2 mm).
  • Welding: SMAW machine, 80-120 A.
  • Grinding: Angle grinder, flap discs (40-600).
  • Template: Cardboard, plastic, or aluminum.
  • Measuring: Calipers, dial indicator.
  • Safety: Welding helmet, respirator (P100), gloves, fire extinguisher.
