WEAR-RATE TRACKING: THE LABYRINTH GAP

Visualizing Runner Drop

As the thrust bearing wears or the foundation settles, the runner vertical position shifts. Simultaneously, suspended silt erodes the labyrinth seal clearances.

[GRAPH: Labyrinth Seal Clearance (mm) vs. Operating Hours] Trend: Linear increase from 0.50mm to 0.85mm over 15,000 hrs

Decision Trigger:

  • Baseline (New): ~0.50 mm
  • Warning Zone: > 1.00 mm (Efficiency drops ~1-2%)
  • FAILURE LIMIT: > 1.50 mm (Excessive leakage causes axial thrust imbalance)

THE SILT CORRELATION: ENVIRONMENT VS. MACHINE

Linking Cause and Effect

In Rwanda, the river conditions dictate machine life. We must correlate vibration data with turbidity data.

[GRAPH: Turbidity (NTU) vs. Shaft Vibration (¬µm)] Visual: Vibration spikes occur 24-48 hours AFTER Turbidity spikes.

The Insight:

  • High Silt (>1000 NTU) enters the machine.
  • Primary Effect: Scours the seal rings (Clearance opens).
  • Secondary Effect: Unbalanced leakage creates hydraulic instability.
  • Lagging Indicator: Vibration rises 2 days later as the damage stabilizes.

Strategy: If Turbidity > 800 NTU, REDUCE LOAD to 70% to lower velocity and impact energy of particles.
