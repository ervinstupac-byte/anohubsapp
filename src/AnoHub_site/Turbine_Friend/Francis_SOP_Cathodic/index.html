The Battery Effect: Dissimilar Metals

When two different metals are submerged in water (an electrolyte), they form a galvanic cell‚Äîessentially a battery.

How It Works

Anode (Sacrificial Metal) ‚Üí Oxidation ‚Üí Electrons Flow ‚Üí Cathode (Protected Metal)
  • Spiral Case Material: Typically Carbon Steel or Cast Iron (less noble).
  • Runner Material: Stainless Steel 13Cr4Ni or Martensitic Stainless (more noble).
  • The Problem: Without protection, the spiral case becomes the anode and corrodes rapidly, especially in aggressive water (high conductivity, low pH, or dissolved oxygen).

Sacrificial Anodes: Inspection Protocol

Step 1: Locate All Anodes

Typical Francis turbine installations have anodes in:

  • Spiral Case: 4-8 anodes distributed around the circumference.
  • Draft Tube: 2-4 anodes near the cone section.
  • Stay Ring: Additional anodes if the stay ring is carbon steel.

Step 2: Visual Inspection

  1. Surface Condition: Anodes should show white/gray corrosion products (zinc oxide/hydroxide).
  2. Measure Remaining Mass: Use calipers or a ruler to measure the remaining thickness/diameter.
  3. Document: Take photos and record dimensions for each anode.

Step 3: Connection Integrity Check

The anode MUST have a clean, metal-to-metal connection to the turbine body.

  • Inspect Mounting Bolt: Check for corrosion, looseness, or paint/coating on contact surfaces.
  • Continuity Test: Use a multimeter (resistance mode) to verify electrical continuity between the anode and the turbine casing (should be < 1 Œ©).
  • Clean Contact Surfaces: Use a wire brush to remove any oxide layer, paint, or debris from the mounting surface.
‚ö†Ô∏è CRITICAL: If the anode is electrically isolated (painted surface, corroded bolt, loose connection), it will NOT protect the turbine. The runner will become the anode and corrode instead.
The 50% Rule: Replacement Criteria

Why 50%?

Anodes are designed with a safety margin. Once 50% of the original mass is consumed:

  • Reduced Surface Area: The anode's ability to protect decreases exponentially.
  • Increased Resistance: Corrosion products build up, reducing electrical contact.
  • Risk of Detachment: Mechanical integrity is compromised‚Äîthe anode may fall off.

Replacement Procedure

  1. Remove Old Anode: Unbolt and discard the consumed anode.
  2. Clean Mounting Surface: Wire brush the contact area to bare metal (remove all paint, rust, and corrosion products).
  3. Install New Anode: Use a new stainless steel bolt with a star washer to ensure tight contact.
  4. Torque Specification: Tighten to manufacturer's spec (typically 40-60 Nm for M12 bolts).
  5. Verify Continuity: Re-test with multimeter (< 1 Œ©).
If Remaining Mass ‚â§ 50% ‚Üí REPLACE IMMEDIATELY
üí° Tech Note: If you delay replacement beyond 50%, the runner becomes the "sacrificial anode" and will develop pitting corrosion on the blade surfaces‚Äîrequiring expensive welding repairs.
Anode Continuity: The Metal-to-Metal Rule

Why Continuity Matters

For cathodic protection to work, electrons must flow freely from the anode to the turbine structure.

Common Failure Modes

  • Painted Surfaces: Paint acts as an insulator. Always remove paint from contact areas.
  • Corroded Bolts: Rust on the mounting bolt creates high resistance. Use stainless steel bolts.
  • Loose Connection: Vibration can loosen bolts over time. Use lock washers or Loctite.
  • Oxide Layer: Aluminum oxide or zinc oxide can form a non-conductive layer. Clean before installation.

Continuity Test Procedure

  1. Set multimeter to resistance (Œ©) mode.
  2. Place one probe on the anode surface.
  3. Place the other probe on the turbine casing (bare metal).
  4. Pass Criteria: Resistance < 1 Œ©.
  5. Fail Criteria: Resistance > 5 Œ© or "OL" (open circuit).

Materials & Tools Checklist
  • Replacement Anodes: Zinc (Zn) or Magnesium (Mg) anodes per OEM specification.
  • Hardware: Stainless steel bolts (M12 or M16), star washers, Loctite 243 (Blue).
  • Tools: Wire brush, calipers, multimeter, torque wrench.
  • Safety: Confined space entry kit, gas detector, ventilation fan.
  • Documentation: Camera, inspection log, anode consumption chart.
Inspection Schedule
  • Annual Inspection: Visual check during scheduled outage.
  • Bi-Annual Measurement: Measure remaining mass and document consumption rate.
  • Immediate Replacement: If any anode reaches 50% consumption.
  • Emergency Check: If unusual corrosion is observed on the runner or spiral case.
