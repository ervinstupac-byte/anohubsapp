'Hollow' Test: Acoustic Concrete Verification

Why Foundation Integrity Matters

The turbine and generator are mounted on a concrete foundation with grout filling the gap between equipment feet and floor. If grout detaches or creates air pockets:

  • Vibration Amplification: Loss of stiffness allows vibration increase.
  • Loss of Alignment: Equipment can shift, causing shaft imbalance.
  • Stress Concentration: Uneven support creates high stress points on the frame.
  • Bearing Failure: Misalignment accelerates bearing wear.

Hammer Test Procedure

  1. Select Hammer: Use a small locksmith hammer (200-300g) or geological hammer.
  2. Identify Zones: Focus on grout around turbine feet, generator, and anchor bolt plates.
  3. Tap Systematically: Tap the grout surface in a grid pattern (every 10-15 cm).
  4. Listen to Sound:
    • Solid Grout: High-frequency, sharp "ting" sound.
    • Delaminated/Hollow: Low-frequency, dull "thud" or "hollow" sound.
  5. Mark Defects: Use chalk to mark all hollow areas.

  • Sketch map of turbine/generator foundation.
  • Mark all hollow areas with dimensions.
  • Photograph marked areas.
  • Estimate total affected area (m¬≤).
Anchor Bolt Tightening: Torque Protocol

Why Anchor Bolts Loosen

Anchor bolts hold the turbine and generator to the foundation. Over time they can loosen due to:

  • Vibration: Constant vibrations cause micro-movements loosening nuts.
  • Thermal Cycles: Temperature changes cause expansion/contraction.
  • Grout Settling: If grout cracks or settles, bolt tension is lost.

Inspection Procedure

  1. Locate All Bolts: Typically 8-16 bolts per unit.
  2. Visual Inspection: Check for rust, bent threads, or missing nuts.
  3. Torque Check: Use calibrated torque wrench to check current state.
  4. Record Values: Document current torque for each bolt.

Tightening Procedure

  1. Determine Target Torque: Consult manufacturer manual. Typical values:
    • M24 bolts: 400-600 Nm
    • M30 bolts: 800-1200 Nm
    • M36 bolts: 1400-2000 Nm
  2. Clean Threads: Clean with wire brush and apply anti-seize paste.
  3. Tighten in Star Pattern: Tighten crosswise for even force distribution.
  4. Multi-Pass Tightening:
    • Pass 1: 50% target torque.
    • Pass 2: 75% target torque.
    • Pass 3: 100% target torque.
  5. Final Verification: Check all bolts again.

Epoxy Injection: Crack Repair

When to Use Epoxy

Injection is used to fill cracks and restore integrity when:

  • Hollow areas detected by hammer test.
  • Visible cracks in concrete (width > 0.5 mm).
  • Grout has detached from foundation.

Epoxy Selection

Type: Use structural concrete resin (e.g. Sikadur, Hilti HIT-RE 500).

Properties:

  • Low viscosity (for deep penetration)
  • High compressive strength (> 70 MPa)
  • Good adhesion to concrete and steel
  • Low shrinkage during curing

Injection Procedure

  1. Surface Preparation:
    • Clean all loose material, dust, and oil.
    • Blow out with compressed air.
  2. Drilling Ports:
    • Drill holes (10-12 mm) spaced 15-30 cm along crack.
    • Drill at 45¬∞ angle into crack.
  3. Packer Installation:
    • Insert plastic or metal packers.
    • Seal around packers with fast-setting putty.
  4. Injecting:
    • Use injection pump.
    • Start from lowest point.
    • Inject until resin exits next packer.
    • Move to next packer.
üí° Technical Note: For large hollow areas (> 0.5 m¬≤), consider removing and repouring grout instead of injecting.
Inspection Schedule
  • Annual Inspection: Hammer test all grouts during overhaul.
  • Bi-Annual Bolt Check: Check anchor bolt torque every 2 years.
  • After Vibration: If abnormal vibrations detected, inspect foundation immediately.
  • After Earthquake: Inspect foundation after any seismic activity (> 4.0).
