FRANCIS HORIZONTAL TURBINE - CRITICAL RESPONSE PROCEDURES

üö® THIS DOCUMENT CONTAINS TIME-CRITICAL PROCEDURES. FAMILIARIZE YOURSELF WITH ALL PROTOCOLS BEFORE EMERGENCY OCCURS. RESPONSE TIME MEASURED IN SECONDS, NOT MINUTES.
PROTOCOL 1: Load Rejection & Overspeed

Scenario: Grid Trip - Unit Goes to Overspeed

Context: If the electrical grid disconnects suddenly (breaker trip, line fault), the turbine loses its electrical load instantly. The runner, which was spinning at rated speed (600 RPM), suddenly has no resistance and begins to accelerate (overspeed).

IMMEDIATE DANGER: If overspeed exceeds 120% rated speed (~720 RPM), centrifugal forces can cause catastrophic runner failure (blade separation). Governor MUST close guide vanes within 3-5 seconds.

Automatic Response Sequence

  1. Grid Relay Detects Disconnect: Loss of voltage or frequency deviation triggers emergency closure signal to governor.
  2. Governor Commands "Full Close": HPU drives servomotor to close all 20 guide vanes to minimum flow position.
  3. Overspeed Protection: If speed reaches 110% rated, emergency overspeed trip activates mechanical brake (if equipped).
  4. Unit Coasts Down: With flow stopped, runner decelerates naturally over 2-5 minutes depending on inertia.
CRITICAL TIMING:
0-2 sec: Grid disconnect detected ‚Üí Governor signal sent
2-5 sec: Guide vanes fully closed ‚Üí Flow cut off
5-10 sec: Speed peaks at ~105-110% ‚Üí Begins deceleration
2-5 min: Unit reaches full stop

Manual Intervention Protocol

IF AUTOMATIC GOVERNOR FAILS TO RESPOND:

  1. Immediate Manual Override: Press "EMERGENCY CLOSE" button on control panel (bypasses all logic, drives HPU to full close).
  2. Monitor Overspeed: Watch tachometer - if speed exceeds 115%, activate EMERGENCY STOP (mechanical brake + MIV emergency closure).
  3. MIV Backup: If guide vanes fail to close and speed rising, manually trigger MIV closure using emergency release (gravity drop or counterweight system).
  4. Evacuation Protocol: If speed exceeds 120% rated and showing no sign of reduction, evacuate powerhouse immediately (runner failure risk).

Post-Event Inspection

  • Inspect runner for cracks (focus on blade roots - highest stress during overspeed)
  • Check governor response time log in SCADA
  • Verify all guide vanes closed synchronously (check eccentric pins if uneven)
PROTOCOL 2: Silt Flash Flood

Scenario: River Silt Levels Exceed Design Limit

Context: During heavy monsoon or upstream landslide, river silt concentration can spike from normal ~500 ppm to >5000 ppm. At these levels, silt acts as liquid sandpaper, eroding runner blades, guide vanes, and bearing seals at an accelerated rate.

DAMAGE MECHANISM: Each hour of operation in high-silt conditions removes 0.1-0.5mm of material from runner blade leading edges. This is equivalent to 6-12 months of normal wear. Runner can become unserviceable in days.

Silt Level Monitoring

Silt Concentration Status Action
< 1000 ppm NORMAL Continue operation
1000-3000 ppm WARNING Increase monitoring frequency, prepare for shutdown
3000-5000 ppm CRITICAL Reduce load to minimum, plan shutdown within 2 hours
> 5000 ppm EMERGENCY IMMEDIATE SHUTDOWN - Do not delay

Emergency Shutdown Procedure

  1. Notify Dispatcher: Inform grid operator of imminent shutdown (avoid grid instability).
  2. Reduce Load Gradually: Over 5-10 minutes, ramp down to minimum generation (prevents thermal shock to runner).
  3. Trip Unit: Open main breaker, allow governor to close guide vanes normally.
  4. Close MIV: After unit stopped, close Main Inlet Valve to prevent silt-laden water from pooling in spiral casing.
  5. Drain Spiral Casing (if possible): Use drain valves to remove silt-laden water before it settles and hardens.

Restart Criteria

DO NOT RESTART UNTIL:

  • River silt levels drop below 2000 ppm (verified by water sample analysis)
  • Intake trash racks cleaned and inspected
  • Spiral casing flushed if drainage was performed
  • Estimated silt flood duration passed (typically 12-48 hours after heavy rain stops)
ECONOMIC DECISION: Shutting down for 24 hours costs ~$X in lost revenue. Replacing eroded runner costs $XXX,XXX and 3-6 months downtime. Shutdown is ALWAYS the correct choice.
PROTOCOL 3: HPU Power Loss - Manual MIV Closure

Scenario: Hydraulic Power Unit Fails During Operation

Context: The HPU provides pressurized oil to operate the guide vane servomotor and (in some designs) the MIV. If HPU loses power or pump fails, there is NO hydraulic pressure to close valves.

RISK: Without guide vane control, turbine operates in "runaway" mode - speed limited only by water flow. If MIV cannot close manually, unit may need to run until upstream reservoir is drained or penstock manually valved off.

Failure Detection

  • HPU pressure gauge drops to zero
  • HPU motor overload trip or electrical fault alarm
  • Guide vanes fail to respond to governor commands
  • Unusual speed fluctuation (loss of governor control)

Manual MIV Closure Procedure

Design-Specific: MIV closure mechanism varies. Two common designs:

Design A: Gravity/Counterweight System

  1. Access MIV Control Station: Located near valve housing, typically in penstock gallery.
  2. Release Hydraulic Lock: Pull emergency release lever (red handle) - this vents hydraulic pressure holding valve open.
  3. Gravity Closure: Heavy MIV disc falls closed under its own weight + counterweight assistance. Closure time: 30-60 seconds.
  4. Verify Closure: Check position indicator shows "CLOSED". Listen for water flow - should cease.
  5. Manual Lock: Engage mechanical locking pin to prevent accidental reopening.

Design B: Manual Hand-Wheel System

  1. Access Hand-Wheel: Typically on top of valve housing or via gearbox on side.
  2. Disengage Hydraulic Drive: Pull clutch lever to disconnect hydraulic actuator from valve stem.
  3. Manual Cranking: Turn hand-wheel clockwise (typically 50-100 full rotations for full closure). Requires 2-3 operators, 5-10 minutes of sustained effort.
  4. Mark Progress: Every 10 rotations, check position indicator to verify valve movement.
  5. Final Seating: Last 10% of closure requires higher torque - use cheater bar if needed (keep spares nearby).
CRITICAL WARNING: Never attempt to manually close MIV while unit is at high speed. Flow forces can make valve impossible to move and may damage manual override mechanism. Always trip unit electrically first if possible.

HPU Redundancy Check

Preventive Measure: During quarterly maintenance, test manual MIV override to ensure it functions:

  • Verify emergency release lever moves freely (not seized)
  • Test hand-wheel rotation (unit stopped, MIV at mid-position)
  • Check counterweights for corrosion or cable fraying
PROTOCOL 4: Linkage Failure - Mechanical Lockout

Scenario: Governor Linkage Shear Pin Breaks

Context: The linkage connecting the servomotor to the regulating ring uses deliberate "shear pins" (sacrificial bolts designed to break at a specific load). If regulating ring jams (debris, bent vane), the shear pin breaks to protect the servomotor from damage.

CONSEQUENCE: With shear pin broken, servomotor can stroke but regulating ring does NOT move. Guide vanes frozen at last position. If frozen at high flow (>60% open), unit continues generating but cannot modulate power.

Failure Symptoms

  • Governor commands position change but power output unchanged
  • Visual inspection: Servomotor piston extending/retracting but regulating ring stationary
  • Loud metallic "clunk" sound at moment of shear pin failure
  • HPU pressure normal (pin failure, not hydraulic issue)

Emergency Response - Mechanical Lock Installation

Objective: Prevent regulating ring from sudden uncontrolled movement if jam clears.

  1. Power Down Unit: Trip breaker, allow unit to coast to stop. DO NOT attempt repair while spinning.
  2. Release HPU Pressure: Vent hydraulic system to zero pressure (prevents servomotor from moving during repair).
  3. Access Regulating Ring: Remove inspection covers for linkage mechanism.
  4. Install Mechanical Lock Pin: Drive locking pin through pre-drilled holes in regulating ring and stationary housing (typically 25mm diameter steel pin, kept with emergency tools).
  5. Secondary Lock: Install redundant locking pin at 180¬∞ opposite position for safety.
  6. Tag Out Servomotor: Red-tag HPU control panel: "LINKAGE FAILURE - DO NOT OPERATE".

Root Cause Investigation

Before Replacing Shear Pin:

  • Manually rotate regulating ring through full travel (0-100%) - identify binding point
  • Inspect all 20 guide vane stems for bent condition or debris jam
  • Check for foreign object (rock, wood) lodged in spiral casing interfering with vane movement
  • Verify servomotor force did not exceed design limit (hydraulic pressure spike?)

Shear Pin Replacement

CRITICAL SPECIFICATION: Shear pins are calibrated to specific break force. NEVER substitute with standard bolt. Using over-strength bolt defeats safety function and will destroy servomotor if jam recurs.
  1. Clear jam / debris causing regulating ring binding
  2. Remove broken shear pin remnants
  3. Install OEM-specified shear pin (keep spares in stock)
  4. Torque to spec (typically hand-tight only - pin designed to shear,not hold torque)
  5. Remove mechanical lock pins
  6. Test linkage by manually stroking servomotor (HPU at low pressure - 50 bar)
